Foundations of Leadership

Learning Objective

Understand what leadership truly means, distinguish it from management, and identify your natural leadership style.

Leadership vs. Management

Management is about coping with complexity — planning, budgeting, organizing, controlling. Leadership is about coping with change — setting direction, aligning people, motivating and inspiring. Working professionals often step into leadership without realizing the shift in mindset required: from 'doing the work' to 'enabling others to do the work.'

The Four Foundational Leadership Styles

1) Directive — clear instructions, useful in crises or with new team members. 2) Participative — invites input, builds buy-in, works well with experienced teams. 3) Delegative — hands over ownership, ideal for high-trust, high-skill teams. 4) Servant — leader supports the team's growth first; builds long-term loyalty. Most effective leaders flex between these based on the situation and the person.

Leadership is a Behaviour, Not a Title

You do not need a designation to lead. Leadership shows up in how you take ownership of problems, how you support colleagues under pressure, and how you communicate under uncertainty. Positional authority can be given or taken away; earned influence cannot.

The Trust Equation

People follow leaders they trust. Trust = Credibility + Reliability + Intimacy, divided by Self-Orientation. In practice: keep your commitments (reliability), be honest even when it's inconvenient (credibility), be approachable (intimacy), and focus on others' interests, not just your own advancement (low self-orientation).
