Change Management & Leading Through Uncertainty

Learning Objective

Lead your team through organizational change and uncertainty with clarity and resilience.

Why People Resist Change

Resistance is rarely about the change itself — it's usually about loss of control, unclear impact on the individual, or past experiences with poorly managed change. Naming these underlying fears is more effective than simply repeating the business case for change.

Kotter's 8-Step Model (Simplified for Team Leaders)

Create urgency, build a coalition of support, form a clear vision, communicate it repeatedly, remove obstacles, generate short-term wins, keep pushing, and anchor the new way of working into team norms. Most change efforts fail from skipping short-term wins and declaring victory too early.

The Leader's Role During Ambiguity

When the full picture isn't yet clear, leaders should be transparent about what is and isn't known, avoid false certainty, and focus the team on what can be controlled in the immediate term, rather than speculating endlessly about the unknown.

Building Team Resilience

Resilient teams are built before the crisis, not during it — through trust, clear communication habits, and psychological safety established in calmer times. Leaders who only invest in relationships during a crisis usually find the well is dry.
