Real-Life Example: The Vague Goal That Finally Became Real

After a leadership workshop, Anand wrote in his notebook: "Be a more patient leader." Six months later, nothing had changed — the goal was too vague to act on. After a second training, he tried again, differently: "In the next 30 days, when a team member makes a mistake in a meeting, I will wait until after the meeting to give feedback privately, instead of correcting them in front of others." This was specific, observable, and time-bound. His team noticed the change within two weeks, and one team member told him directly that meetings felt safer now.
