Effective Communication for Leaders

ContentReal-Life ExamplesMetaphorWorksheetAction Plan
Learning Objective

Master clear, confident communication that builds trust and drives action.

The Leader's Communication Responsibility

As a leader, you are responsible not just for what you say, but for what is heard and understood. Ambiguity that seems harmless to you can create confusion, rework, or anxiety downstream. Precision is a form of respect for your team's time.

Active Listening as a Leadership Tool

Listening to respond is different from listening to understand. Techniques: paraphrase what you heard before replying, ask one clarifying question before offering a solution, and resist the urge to interrupt with your own experience.

Giving Feedback That Lands

Effective feedback is specific, timely, and behaviour-focused (not personality-focused). The SBI model helps: Situation (when/where), Behaviour (what you observed), Impact (the effect it had). This keeps feedback factual and reduces defensiveness.

Communicating Up, Down, and Across

The message is the same but the framing changes: with senior leadership, lead with the bottom line and business impact; with your team, lead with context and the 'why'; with peers, lead with shared goals and collaboration, not competition.
