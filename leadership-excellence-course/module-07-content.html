Building & Leading High-Performance Teams

Learning Objective

Understand team dynamics and apply practical techniques to build cohesion and performance.

Tuckman's Stages of Team Development

Forming (polite, cautious) → Storming (conflict and testing boundaries) → Norming (agreements form) → Performing (high trust, high output) → Adjourning (wrap-up). Storming is uncomfortable but necessary — teams that avoid it never truly reach Performing.

Psychological Safety as the Foundation

Google's Project Aristotle research found psychological safety — the shared belief that it's safe to take interpersonal risks — was the single biggest predictor of team effectiveness, more than individual talent. Leaders build this by responding to mistakes and dissent with curiosity, not punishment.

Clarity of Roles Reduces Friction

Ambiguous role boundaries are a leading cause of team conflict, often mistaken for personality clashes. A simple RACI exercise (who is Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed) for key processes resolves much of this friction quickly.

Celebrating Progress, Not Just Outcomes

High-performing teams get recognition for effort and improvement along the way, not only for final results. This sustains motivation on long projects and reduces burnout during periods where outcomes aren't yet visible.
