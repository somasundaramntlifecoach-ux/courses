Personal Leadership Action Plan & Continuous Growth

Learning Objective

Consolidate your learning into a concrete, personal leadership development plan.

Why Most Development Plans Fail

Vague intentions ('be a better communicator') rarely change behaviour. Specific, observable, time-bound commitments ('in team meetings this month, I will pause 3 seconds before responding to disagreement') are far more likely to stick, because they're easy to notice and self-check.

The 70-20-10 Model for Leadership Growth

Roughly 70% of leadership growth happens through on-the-job experience and stretch assignments, 20% through feedback and relationships (mentors, peers), and 10% through formal training like this course. This course is most valuable when paired with real application in your role.

Choosing Your Top 2-3 Priorities

Trying to change everything from all 14 modules at once leads to changing nothing. Reviewing your reflections from each module, identify the 2-3 leadership behaviours that would have the highest impact on your team if improved in the next 90 days.

Building an Accountability Loop

Growth sticks when there's a feedback loop: share your development priorities with your manager or a peer, set a specific check-in date, and ask a trusted colleague to give you honest signal on whether they've noticed the change.
