Ethical Leadership & Integrity

ContentReal-Life ExamplesMetaphorWorksheetAction Plan
Learning Objective

Lead with integrity, make ethically sound decisions, and build a culture of trust.

Why Ethics is a Leadership Skill, Not Just a Personal Virtue

A leader's ethical choices are amplified — your team watches what you do far more than what you say, and they calibrate their own standards accordingly. Small compromises ('just this once') are rarely isolated; they set a precedent the team notices.

A Simple Ethical Decision Test

Before a decision: 1) Is it legal? 2) Does it align with our stated values? 3) Would I be comfortable if this decision were made public? 4) How would I feel if someone did this to me or my team? If any answer gives pause, slow down before proceeding.

Handling Ethical Grey Areas

Not every dilemma has a clean answer. When principles conflict (e.g., loyalty to a struggling team member vs. fairness to the whole team), name the tension explicitly rather than pretending there's an easy answer, and involve trusted advisors or HR where appropriate.

Building a Speak-Up Culture

People only report concerns, mistakes, or ethical grey areas if they trust the response won't be punitive. Leaders build this by responding to bad news calmly, thanking people for raising issues (even inconvenient ones), and visibly following through on what they said they'd do about it.
