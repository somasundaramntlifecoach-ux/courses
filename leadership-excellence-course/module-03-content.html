Vision & Goal Setting

ContentReal-Life ExamplesMetaphorWorksheetAction Plan
Learning Objective

Learn to craft a compelling vision and translate it into clear, achievable goals for your team.

Why Vision Matters

A vision answers 'why does this work matter?' Teams with a clear sense of purpose outperform teams that only receive task lists, because purpose sustains motivation when tasks get repetitive or difficult. A good vision is short enough to remember and specific enough to guide decisions.

From Vision to Strategy to Goals

Vision is the destination. Strategy is the broad route. Goals are the concrete milestones along that route. Leaders who skip straight to goals without connecting them to a larger 'why' often see compliance, not commitment.

SMART and Beyond: OKRs

SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) remain useful for individual tasks. For team-level alignment, many organizations now use OKRs (Objectives and Key Results) — an ambitious Objective paired with 3-5 measurable Key Results that indicate progress.

Communicating Goals So They Stick

Repetition is not nagging — it's reinforcement. Effective leaders repeat the 'why' behind goals in different formats: team meetings, 1:1s, written recaps. People need to hear the purpose multiple times, in multiple ways, before it truly sinks in and shapes behaviour.
