Module 8: Navigating Office Politics

Page 2 of 3 · Practical Guidance & Tips

Step-by-Step Guidance

  1. List the 3-5 people who are actually consulted before major decisions in your area, regardless of title.
  2. For each, note whether you currently have any working relationship with them at all.
  3. Choose one to build a small, genuine connection with this month.
  4. Notice the next time you're tempted to join a purely complaint-focused conversation, and offer a solution-oriented alternative instead.
  5. Deliberately avoid becoming known as aligned with only one 'side' in any ongoing team tension.

DO

  • Build working relationships with informal influencers, not just people with senior titles.
  • Bring at least one possible solution whenever you raise a problem.
  • Stay visibly fair and useful across different stakeholders and teams.

DON'T

  • Don't ignore the informal power structure just because it feels uncomfortable or 'political'.
  • Don't participate in gossip or complaint-only conversations, even when everyone else is.
  • Don't become known as permanently loyal to one clique or faction.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Assuming the official org chart reflects who actually influences decisions.
  • Avoiding necessary relationships entirely because they feel 'too political', at real cost to your effectiveness.
  • Building a reputation as someone who only raises problems, never solutions.
SAMPLE SCRIPT

“Before we finalise this, I wanted to get your read on it early, since I know you often have visibility into how this plays out — does this direction make sense from where you sit?”
