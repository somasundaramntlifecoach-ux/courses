Module 9: Building Your Promotion Case

Page 2 of 3 · Practical Guidance & Tips

Step-by-Step Guidance

  1. Start or update a running achievement log today, adding entries as they happen going forward.
  2. Convert your three strongest recent achievements into STAR format, each with a number attached wherever possible.
  3. Draft the specific role or level you want to be considered for, in one clear sentence.
  4. Write and rehearse your opening line for the actual promotion conversation.
  5. Schedule the conversation with your manager within a specific, committed timeframe — not 'sometime soon'.

DO

  • Attach a number to every achievement you can, even if it's a reasonable estimate.
  • Keep your achievement log updated weekly or biweekly, not just before appraisals.
  • State the specific role or level you want clearly, rather than hinting at general ambition.

DON'T

  • Don't wait until the appraisal window to start compiling your case — start the log continuously.
  • Don't rely on vague descriptions like 'worked hard' or 'contributed significantly' without specifics.
  • Don't leave the actual promotion conversation unscheduled indefinitely out of discomfort.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Trying to recall an entire year's achievements from memory in one sitting right before a review.
  • Presenting achievements without any quantified impact attached.
  • Having all the evidence ready but never actually having the direct conversation.
SAMPLE SCRIPT

“Based on the impact I've delivered over the last [period] — including [top 2-3 STAR achievements] — I believe I'm ready to be considered for [specific role/level]. I'd like to understand what the next steps would look like.”
