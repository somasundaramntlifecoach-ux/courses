Module 4: Closing Your Skill Gaps

Page 1 of 3 · Concepts & Real-Life Examples
If your diagnostic pointed toward a genuine skill gap, this module gives you a structured way to identify exactly what's missing and close it efficiently — instead of vaguely 'upskilling' in whatever direction feels safe or fashionable, which is how many professionals waste a year on the wrong certification.

Shadow the Role Above You

Ask to sit in on a meeting, review a document, or shadow a task typically done one level up. This single action reveals the real, not assumed, skill requirements far faster than any job description.

Competency Mapping

List the 4-5 core competencies of the next role and honestly rate your current level in each on a simple 1-5 scale. This turns a vague feeling of 'not ready yet' into a specific, actionable list.

The 70-20-10 Model

Roughly 70% of real skill-building happens through on-the-job stretch assignments, 20% through mentoring and direct feedback, and only about 10% through formal courses or certifications. Most people invert this ratio and over-invest in courses alone.

Targeted, Not Generic, Learning

A certification only helps if it closes a gap someone has actually flagged for you — not just any credential that looks impressive on paper but doesn't address what's actually holding you back.

REAL-LIFE EXAMPLE

Karthik, Client Delivery Lead

Karthik assumed his gap was technical delivery skill and spent months preparing detailed technical documentation nobody had asked for. After finally asking to shadow his manager on one client negotiation call, he discovered the real gap was commercial negotiation — an entirely different skill he'd never practiced.

REAL-LIFE EXAMPLE

Priya, Operations Analyst

Priya deliberately applied the 70-20-10 model: she took one stretch project (the 70%), found an informal mentor two levels above her for regular feedback (the 20%), and completed one focused certification directly relevant to a flagged gap (the 10%). She was promoted in eight months, compared to three stalled years before that.
