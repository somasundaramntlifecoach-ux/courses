Module 4: Closing Your Skill Gaps

Page 2 of 3 · Practical Guidance & Tips

Step-by-Step Guidance

  1. Identify the role one level above yours and list its 4-5 core competencies.
  2. Rate your current level honestly, 1-5, in each competency.
  3. Ask to shadow, observe, or review work from that next-level role at least once.
  4. For your single lowest-rated competency, identify one stretch assignment that would build it directly.
  5. Only after that, consider whether a specific course or certification would close a remaining, clearly-identified gap.

DO

  • Ask directly what separates you from the next level, rather than guessing.
  • Prioritise stretch assignments over courses when both are available.
  • Revisit your competency map every 6 months — requirements shift as roles evolve.

DON'T

  • Don't sign up for a broad certification hoping it addresses an unspecified gap.
  • Don't skip the shadowing step — it's the fastest way to correct wrong assumptions.
  • Don't try to close every competency gap at once; focus on the one or two lowest first.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Assuming the skill gap is technical when it's actually commercial, interpersonal, or strategic.
  • Over-investing in the 10% (formal courses) while neglecting the 70% (stretch assignments).
  • Waiting to be assigned a stretch project instead of asking for one directly.
SAMPLE SCRIPT

“I'd like to build toward [next role]. Would it be possible for me to shadow or sit in on [specific meeting/task] so I understand what that level actually requires day to day?”
