Module 2: The Promotion Equation

Page 2 of 3 · Practical Guidance & Tips

Step-by-Step Guidance

  1. Score yourself honestly, 1-10, on Performance, Perception, and Positioning separately.
  2. Multiply the three scores together to get your real readiness number.
  3. Identify which single factor is dragging your total down the most.
  4. Choose one concrete action this month that specifically targets that weakest factor.
  5. Re-score yourself in 90 days to check whether the weakest factor has moved.

DO

  • Score yourself as if you were a neutral outside observer, not as you'd like to be seen.
  • Focus your energy on the single lowest-scoring factor rather than spreading effort evenly.
  • Revisit your scores periodically — they shift as circumstances change.

DON'T

  • Don't assume a high Performance score compensates for a near-zero elsewhere — the multiplication punishes that assumption.
  • Don't skip scoring Positioning just because it feels less concrete than the other two.
  • Don't treat this as a one-time exercise; readiness is dynamic.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Only tracking performance metrics (appraisal scores, KPIs) and ignoring perception or positioning entirely.
  • Assuming perception automatically follows from performance without any deliberate effort.
  • Never having the actual promotion conversation and simply hoping it happens organically.
SAMPLE SCRIPT

“If I imagine my Performance, how people perceive that performance, and how well I'm positioned for promotion as three separate scores out of 10 — which one would genuinely pull my average down the most?”
