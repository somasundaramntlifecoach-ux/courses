Module 8: Navigating Office Politics

Page 1 of 3 · Concepts & Real-Life Examples
"Office politics" carries a negative reputation, but at its core it's simply an honest understanding of how influence and decisions actually flow in your organisation — which is often quite different from the official org chart. This module reframes politics as a skill you can practice with integrity, not a game you either play dirty or avoid entirely.

Politics as Influence, Not Manipulation

Understanding who actually shapes decisions, and building genuine working relationships with them, isn't manipulative — ignoring this reality simply makes you less effective, not more principled.

Map the Informal Power Structure

Identify who is genuinely consulted before big decisions, regardless of their formal title. These are often the people most worth building an honest working relationship with.

Build Alliances, Not Sides

Stay useful and fair to multiple stakeholders rather than becoming known as permanently aligned with one faction or clique — that reputation limits your options later.

Stay Solution-Focused

Avoid gossip and complaint-only conversations. Being known consistently as the person who brings solutions, not just problems, builds durable political capital over time.

REAL-LIFE EXAMPLE

Manoj, Supply Chain Manager

Manoj noticed that budget decisions in his division were really shaped by the Finance Controller, not just his own VP as the org chart suggested. He started proactively copying the Controller on relevant updates and asking for her input early in the process — which noticeably smoothed budget approvals that had previously stalled for weeks.

REAL-LIFE EXAMPLE

Swathi, Program Manager

Instead of joining her team's regular complaint session about a difficult partner department, Swathi offered instead to set up a joint problem-solving call between the two teams. Over time, this earned her a reputation as a bridge-builder rather than a complainer — a reputation that reached senior leadership.
