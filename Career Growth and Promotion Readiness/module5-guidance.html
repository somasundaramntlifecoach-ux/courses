Module 5: Executive Presence

Page 2 of 3 · Practical Guidance & Tips

Step-by-Step Guidance

  1. Before your next senior meeting, write your conclusion first, then the 2-3 supporting points — practice saying it in that order.
  2. Notice your default reaction to pushback or criticism in meetings this week; write down what you actually do.
  3. Practice one composed response you can use under pressure (see the sample script) until it feels natural.
  4. Audit your last written communication to leadership — does it lead with the point, or build up to it?
  5. Ask one trusted colleague for candid feedback on how you come across in high-stakes meetings.

DO

  • Pause before responding to a difficult question rather than filling the silence immediately.
  • Lead emails and updates to senior audiences with the headline, not the background.
  • Prepare your key point in one sentence before any high-stakes meeting.

DON'T

  • Don't over-explain or over-apologise when challenged — it reads as less confident, not more thorough.
  • Don't bury your main point under three paragraphs of context.
  • Don't mistake talking more for presenting more presence — often it's the opposite.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Treating executive presence as something you either have or don't, rather than a practiceable skill.
  • Confusing loudness or dominance in a room with genuine gravitas.
  • Reacting defensively to challenge instead of responding with composed, specific next steps.
SAMPLE SCRIPT

“That's a fair point — let me look into it specifically and come back to you by [date] with a clear answer, rather than guessing now.”
