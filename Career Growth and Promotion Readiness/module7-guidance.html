Module 7: Managing Upward

Page 2 of 3 · Practical Guidance & Tips

Step-by-Step Guidance

  1. Identify your manager's top 2-3 priorities or pressures this quarter.
  2. Reframe one of your recent contributions explicitly in terms of one of those priorities.
  3. Send one proactive 'heads up' on a potential blocker before it's asked about.
  4. Schedule a specific conversation to directly state your interest in promotion — don't leave it implied.
  5. Ask your manager plainly what the realistic timeline and criteria are for your next step.

DO

  • State your promotion interest explicitly and specifically, naming the role or level.
  • Frame your updates around your manager's priorities, not just your own task list.
  • Flag risks and blockers early, even when they're not fully resolved yet.

DON'T

  • Don't assume silence means your manager already knows you want to be promoted.
  • Don't only communicate with your manager when something is needed from them.
  • Don't let 'it'll come up naturally' substitute for an actual scheduled conversation.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Leaving promotion interest permanently implied instead of ever stating it directly.
  • Surprising your manager with problems instead of flagging them early.
  • Not knowing your organisation's actual promotion cycle or criteria and assuming a vague timeline.
SAMPLE SCRIPT

“I wanted to be direct about something — I'm aiming to be considered for [specific role/level] in the next cycle. What would you say I need to demonstrate to get there?”
