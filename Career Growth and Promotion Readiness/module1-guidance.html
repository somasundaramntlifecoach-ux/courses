Module 1: Why You're Stuck

Page 2 of 3 · Practical Guidance & Tips

Step-by-Step Guidance

  1. List every instance in the last 18 months where you were passed over for something — a project, a promotion, a visible assignment.
  2. For each instance, write the *stated* reason (if any was given) and your honest guess at the *real* reason.
  3. Look for a pattern across instances: does it point more toward skill, visibility, or positioning?
  4. Cross-check your pattern against the free assessment results if you haven't already taken it.
  5. Pick the single gap with the strongest evidence and commit to focusing the rest of this course on it first.

DO

  • Ask for specific, concrete feedback rather than accepting vague answers like 'just keep doing what you're doing'.
  • Seek a second opinion from someone who isn't your direct manager.
  • Be willing to hear that your gap isn't the one you assumed.

DON'T

  • Don't default to 'I need another certification' as the first conclusion.
  • Don't rely only on your own self-perception — it's the least reliable data source here.
  • Don't diagnose yourself once and never revisit it; gaps shift as you grow.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Treating all three gaps as equally likely instead of looking for the dominant one.
  • Waiting for a formal appraisal conversation to get this feedback instead of asking proactively.
  • Confusing 'I feel underappreciated' with 'I have a visibility gap' without checking the evidence.
SAMPLE SCRIPT

“I've noticed I wasn't considered for [X] recently, and I want to actually understand why — not to argue about it, just to get better information. Could you help me understand what might be missing?”
