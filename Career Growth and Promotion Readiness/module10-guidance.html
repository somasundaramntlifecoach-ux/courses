Module 10: Life After the Promotion

Page 2 of 3 · Practical Guidance & Tips

Step-by-Step Guidance

  1. Audit which visibility, networking, and upward-management habits from this course you're at risk of quietly dropping now.
  2. Recommit explicitly to at least your weekly update and brag-bank habit at your new level.
  3. Identify one junior colleague you could begin informally mentoring using what you've learned.
  4. Write a specific, dated goal for where you want to be in two years at this new level.
  5. Schedule a personal check-in with yourself in 6 months to catch any early signs of a new plateau forming.

DO

  • Restart your visibility habits immediately at the new level, without waiting for them to be needed again.
  • Look actively for a junior colleague you can mentor using this course's material.
  • Set a specific two-year goal now, while the momentum and clarity from this course are still fresh.

DON'T

  • Don't assume the habits that got you promoted are no longer necessary once you've arrived.
  • Don't wait for a second plateau to form before revisiting these modules.
  • Don't keep everything you've learned to yourself instead of passing it forward.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Treating a promotion as a finish line rather than a new starting point with its own visibility and positioning requirements.
  • Letting go of proactive habits the moment the immediate pressure of the promotion push is over.
  • Never mentoring anyone else, missing an easy source of ongoing credibility and reinforcement.
SAMPLE SCRIPT

“I know what it's like to feel stuck at a level for a while — happy to share what's worked for me if it would be useful for where you're at right now.”
