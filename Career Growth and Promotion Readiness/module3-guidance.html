Module 3: Building Visibility Without Bragging

Page 2 of 3 · Practical Guidance & Tips

Step-by-Step Guidance

  1. Choose a cadence — weekly or biweekly — and a recipient list for your update (manager, skip-level, or both).
  2. Draft a template: 2-3 bullet points, each stating what you did and its measurable impact.
  3. Send your first update this week, even if it feels awkward at first — the discomfort fades by the third or fourth time.
  4. Start a running 'brag bank' document and add to it in the same sitting as your weekly update.
  5. Review your brag bank before every 1:1 and appraisal conversation so nothing gets forgotten.

DO

  • Lead with outcomes and numbers, not effort or hours spent.
  • Credit your team where relevant — it doesn't dilute your visibility, it strengthens your reputation.
  • Keep updates short; three focused bullet points beat a long email nobody fully reads.

DON'T

  • Don't wait for a 'big enough' achievement before starting — consistency matters more than size.
  • Don't frame updates as complaints about workload dressed up as visibility.
  • Don't skip weeks once you start; irregular updates lose most of their compounding value.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Confusing visibility with self-promotion and avoiding it altogether out of discomfort.
  • Sharing effort ('I stayed late all week') instead of outcome ('this reduced turnaround time by two days').
  • Only updating during appraisal season instead of continuously.
SAMPLE SCRIPT

“Quick update from this week: closed out the [X] project, which is expected to save the team roughly [Y] hours a month going forward. Also made progress on [Z], on track for next week.”
